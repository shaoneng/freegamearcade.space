Chess

How to Play Chess

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a checkered board with 64 squares. The main objective is to 'checkmate' the opponent's King, which means putting it in a position where it is under attack (in 'check') and cannot escape capture.

Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. Every piece type has a unique way of moving across the board. For example, Rooks move in straight lines, Bishops move diagonally, and the powerful Queen can move in any straight or diagonal direction.

To play this version, simply click on one of your pieces to see its possible moves highlighted on the board. Then, click on the desired square to move your piece. Plan your attacks, defend your King, and try to outsmart the computer opponent. Take your time, think ahead, and enjoy this classic mental challenge!
