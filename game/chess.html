Chess

How to Play Chess

Welcome to Chess, the legendary game of strategy played for over 1500 years! The objective is simple yet profound: to trap your opponent's King in a position where it cannot escape capture—a move known as "checkmate."

The game is played on an 8x8 checkered board. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one King, one Queen, two Rooks, two Knights, two Bishops, and eight Pawns. Each type of piece has a unique way of moving across the board:

  • The King: Can move one square in any direction. It's the most important piece, but also one of the weakest.
  • The Queen: The most powerful piece, it can move any number of squares in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).
  • The Rooks: Move any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • The Bishops: Move any number of squares diagonally.
  • The Knights: Move in an "L" shape—two squares in one direction and then one square at a right angle. Knights are the only pieces that can jump over other pieces.
  • The Pawns: Move forward one square, but capture diagonally. On their first move, they have the option to move forward two squares.

Players take turns moving one of their pieces to a new square. The game requires careful planning, foresight, and the ability to anticipate your opponent's moves. Outwit your adversary, control the board, and go for the checkmate to claim victory!
