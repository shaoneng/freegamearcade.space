Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The objective in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards from the table. To do this, you must select pairs of cards that add up to a total value of 13.

Card values are straightforward: Number cards are worth their face value (e.g., a 7 is worth 7 points). Face cards have specific values: Jacks are 11, and Queens are 12. Kings are unique, as they are worth 13 on their own and can be removed individually without needing a pair.

You can only play cards that are fully uncovered. Look for available pairs in the pyramid. If you can't find any matches, click on the stock pile to draw a new card. This card can then be paired with any uncovered card in the pyramid. You can cycle through the stock pile as many times as you need.

A good strategy is to prioritize moves that will uncover the most cards, giving you more options for future matches. Think ahead and plan your moves to dismantle the pyramid efficiently!
