Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

Welcome to Pyramid Solitaire, a relaxing and strategic card puzzle. The main goal is to clear a pyramid of cards by finding and matching pairs that total 13.

Card values are straightforward: Kings are worth 13 and can be removed by themselves. Queens are 12, Jacks are 11, and all numbered cards hold their face value (Aces are 1). You can pair any two uncovered cards to make 13, for example, a 6 and a 7, or a Queen and an Ace.

Only cards that are not covered by another card can be selected. Work your way from the bottom layers of the pyramid up. If you run out of available moves on the pyramid, click the stock pile at the bottom to draw a new card. This card can be used to make a pair with any uncovered card in the pyramid. You can cycle through the deck multiple times.

To win, you must clear all the cards from the pyramid. Think strategically! If you have a choice between two possible matches, it's often best to choose the one that will uncover the most new cards, opening up more possibilities for your next move.
