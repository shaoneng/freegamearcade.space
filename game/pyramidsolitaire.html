Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The main objective in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear a pyramid of cards from the table. You achieve this by finding and selecting pairs of uncovered cards that have a combined value of 13.

Card values are straightforward: Number cards are worth their face value (e.g., a 7 is worth 7 points). Jacks are worth 11, Queens are 12, and Aces are 1. Kings are unique; they are worth 13 on their own and can be removed by simply clicking them.

You can only select cards that are fully uncovered and not blocked by other cards in the pyramid. For example, you can pair a 6 with a 7, or a Queen (12) with an Ace (1). The suits of the cards do not matter for making a match.

If you run out of possible matches on the pyramid, you can draw a card from the stockpile at the bottom. This card can be paired with any uncovered card in the pyramid. You can cycle through the deck multiple times if needed. Think strategically to uncover new cards and clear the entire board!
