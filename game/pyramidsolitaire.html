Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The objective of Pyramid Solitaire is to clear a pyramid of cards by matching pairs that sum to a total of 13. This calming card puzzle is easy to learn but offers a satisfying strategic challenge.

Card values are as follows: Kings are worth 13 and can be removed individually. Queens are 12, Jacks are 11, and numbered cards hold their face value (Aces are 1). To remove cards, find two exposed cards that add up to 13, such as a 6 and a 7, or an Ace and a Queen. The suits and colors of the cards do not matter for a match.

You can only select cards that are not covered by any other card. If you cannot find any matches within the pyramid, click the deck to draw a new card. You can cycle through the deck multiple times if needed. To win, you must clear all cards from the pyramid.

A good strategy is crucial. When faced with multiple possible matches, choose the one that uncovers the most new cards. This will open up more options and increase your chances of clearing the entire board. This game is a gentle exercise for your brain, helping to improve attention, mental math, and memory skills.
