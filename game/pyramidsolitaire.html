Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The main objective in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards from the board. You achieve this by finding and selecting pairs of available cards that add up to a total value of 13.

Card values are simple: Numbered cards are worth their face value (e.g., an 8 is worth 8), Aces are worth 1, Jacks are 11, and Queens are 12. Kings are a special case; they are worth 13 on their own, so you can remove a King by simply clicking it without needing a pair.

You can only select cards that are fully uncovered. For example, to match a Queen (12) with an Ace (1), both cards must not be overlapped by any other cards. As you remove pairs, you will reveal the cards underneath, opening up new possibilities.

If you get stuck and can't find any pairs on the pyramid, click the deck at the bottom to draw a new card. You can use this card from the stockpile to make a match. For a winning strategy, try to make moves that expose the most new cards, which will give you more options for future moves.
