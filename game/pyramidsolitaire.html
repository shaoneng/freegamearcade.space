Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

Welcome to Pyramid Solitaire, a classic card puzzle that's both relaxing and mentally stimulating. The main objective is to clear the pyramid of cards by finding pairs that sum to 13.

Card values are as follows: Kings are worth 13 and can be removed individually. Queens are 12, Jacks are 11, and Aces are 1. All other cards hold their face value. You can pair any two uncovered cards, regardless of their suit or color, as long as their values add up to 13 (e.g., a Queen and an Ace, a Jack and a 2, or a 10 and a 3).

Only cards that are not covered by any other card are available to be played. Start by scanning the bottom rows of the pyramid for matches. If you cannot find any more pairs on the board, click the stock pile to draw a new card. You can cycle through the stock pile multiple times. For a better score, try to make moves that uncover the most new cards, opening up more possibilities for future matches.
