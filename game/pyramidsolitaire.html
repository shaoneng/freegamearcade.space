Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The main objective in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards from the table. You accomplish this by finding and selecting pairs of available cards that add up to a total value of 13.

Card values are simple: Kings are worth 13 and can be removed by themselves. Queens are valued at 12, Jacks at 11, and all numbered cards carry their face value (with Aces being 1). You can match any two cards, as their suit and color do not matter. For example, a 10 of Spades can be paired with a 3 of Hearts.

You can only play cards that are fully uncovered in the pyramid. As you remove cards, you will uncover others, making them available to play. If you cannot find any matching pairs on the pyramid, click the deck at the bottom to draw a new card. This card can be paired with any available card in the pyramid. You can cycle through the deck multiple times. For a winning strategy, try to make matches that will open up the most new cards.
