Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The main objective in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards. You accomplish this by selecting pairs of available cards that sum to a total value of 13. Only cards that are not covered by any other card are considered available for play.

Card values are straightforward: Number cards are worth their face value (an Ace is 1, a Two is 2, etc.), Jacks are valued at 11, and Queens are 12. Kings are unique; they are valued at 13 and can be removed individually without needing a pair. The suit or color of the cards does not matter when forming a pair.

If you cannot find any matching pairs within the pyramid, you can draw a card from the stock pile at the bottom. This card can be paired with any available card in the pyramid or from the waste pile. You can cycle through the entire stock pile multiple times if you get stuck.

A key strategy is to plan your moves carefully. When faced with a choice between two possible pairs, always prioritize the one that will uncover the most new cards, thereby increasing your options for future moves and your chances of clearing the board.
