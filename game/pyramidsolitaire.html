Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The goal of Pyramid Solitaire is to clear a pyramid of cards by matching pairs that add up to 13. Only cards that are fully uncovered—not overlapped by any other card—can be selected to make a match.

Card values are simple: Kings are worth 13 and can be removed on their own. Queens are 12, Jacks are 11, and all numbered cards hold their face value, with Aces counting as 1. You can pair any two available cards that total 13, such as a 6 and a 7, or a Jack and a 2.

If you can't find any matches in the pyramid, click the deck at the bottom to draw a new card. You can use this card from the waste pile to make a pair with any uncovered card in the pyramid. You can go through the deck multiple times. For the best chance of winning, try to make matches that will uncover the most new cards, helping you clear the board more efficiently.

Pyramid Solitaire is more than just a game; it's a relaxing mental exercise. It helps improve concentration, mental math skills, and patience. With no timers, you can play at your own pace, making it the perfect way to unwind and de-stress. Enjoy this calming card puzzle and sharpen your mind!
