Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The objective of Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards. To do this, you must find and select pairs of available cards that add up to a total value of 13.

Card values are straightforward: Numbered cards hold their face value (e.g., a 7 is worth 7 points), an Ace is worth 1, a Jack is 11, and a Queen is 12. Kings are a special case, as they are worth 13 on their own and can be removed individually without needing a pair.

You can only select cards that are fully uncovered. Match two available cards from the pyramid, or match a pyramid card with the top card from the stockpile at the bottom. If you run out of moves, click the stockpile to draw a new card. You can cycle through the stockpile as many times as you need.

A key strategy is to always make a move that opens up the most new cards. If you have a choice between two pairs, choose the one that will expose cards deeper in the pyramid, giving you more options for your next turn.
