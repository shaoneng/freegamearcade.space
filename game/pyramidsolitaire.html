Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The main goal in Pyramid Solitaire is to dismantle a pyramid of cards by selecting pairs that add up to a total value of 13. Only cards that are fully uncovered are available to be played.

Card values are straightforward: Number cards hold their face value, Jacks are worth 11, Queens are 12, and Aces are 1. Kings are unique, valued at 13, and can be removed by themselves without needing a pair.

To make a match, simply click on two available cards that sum to 13 (for example, a 6 and a 7, or a Queen and an Ace). You can also match a card from the pyramid with the top card from the draw pile.

If you run out of moves on the board, click the deck at the bottom to draw a new card. You can cycle through the deck multiple times if needed. A good strategy is to prioritize matches that will uncover the most new cards, helping you clear the board faster.
