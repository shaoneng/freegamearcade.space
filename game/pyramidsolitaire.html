Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The goal in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards from the tableau. To do this, you must select pairs of cards that have a total value of 13.

Card values are straightforward: Number cards are worth their face value (e.g., a 7 is worth 7 points). Jacks are worth 11, Queens are 12, and Kings are worth 13. Aces are worth 1. Since Kings are already valued at 13, you can remove them by simply clicking on them without needing a pair.

You can only select cards that are fully uncovered. Match any two uncovered cards that add up to 13, such as a 6 and a 7, or a Jack and a 2. You can also pair a card from the pyramid with a card from the stock pile at the bottom. If you run out of moves on the pyramid, click the stock pile to draw a new card. You can cycle through the stock pile multiple times. Think strategically to uncover new cards and clear the board!
