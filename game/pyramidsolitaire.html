Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The objective of Pyramid Solitaire is to clear a pyramid-shaped layout of cards. You can do this by selecting pairs of available cards that add up to a total of 13. Only cards that are not covered by any other card can be played.

The card values are simple: Kings are worth 13 and can be removed on their own. Queens are worth 12, Jacks are 11, and all number cards (including Aces, which count as 1) hold their face value. For example, you could pair a Queen with an Ace, a Jack with a 2, or an 8 with a 5.

If you run out of possible matches on the pyramid, you can draw a card from the stock pile. The drawn card can be used to form a pair with any uncovered card on the pyramid. You can go through the stock pile multiple times. To win, you must clear all cards from the pyramid.

A good strategy is to prioritize moves that will uncover the most new cards. If you have a choice between two pairs, select the one that opens up more of the pyramid for future plays. This game is a relaxing way to improve focus, practice mental math, and exercise strategic thinking.
