Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The goal of Pyramid Solitaire is to clear the entire pyramid of cards. To do this, you must select pairs of cards that add up to a total of 13.

The card values are straightforward:

  • Kings: Value of 13, and are removed by themselves.
  • Queens: Value of 12.
  • Jacks: Value of 11.
  • Aces: Value of 1.
  • Number Cards (2-10): Face value.

You can only play cards that are fully uncovered. Match two available cards (from the pyramid or the draw pile) that equal 13, for example, a 6 and a 7, or a Queen and an Ace. The suits and colors of the cards do not matter.

If you run out of moves on the pyramid, click the deck at the bottom to draw a new card. You can use this card to make a pair with a card from the pyramid. You can cycle through the entire deck multiple times if needed.

Strategy Tip: If you have multiple pairing options, choose the one that will uncover the most new cards in the pyramid. This will give you more options for your future moves and increase your chances of winning!
