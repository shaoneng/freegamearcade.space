Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The objective in Pyramid Solitaire is to clear a pyramid-shaped layout of cards. You achieve this by selecting pairs of uncovered cards that add up to a total value of 13. Card values are straightforward: Number cards are worth their face value, Jacks are 11, and Queens are 12.

Kings are a special case, as they have a value of 13 by themselves. You can remove a King simply by clicking on it, without needing a pair. For all other cards, find a matching pair (e.g., an 8 and a 5, or a Jack and a 2). You can only select cards that are fully exposed and not covered by any other card in the pyramid.

If you run out of available moves on the pyramid, click on the stock pile at the bottom to draw a new card. This card can be used to make a pair with any uncovered card from the pyramid or the discard pile. For the best results, always try to make a move that will uncover the most new cards, opening up more possibilities for future matches.
