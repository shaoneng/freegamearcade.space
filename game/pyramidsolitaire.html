Pyramid Solitaire

How to Play Pyramid Solitaire

The goal of Pyramid Solitaire is simple yet captivating: clear the pyramid of cards by finding pairs that add up to a total of 13.

Here’s how the card values work:

  • Kings (K) are worth 13 and can be removed by themselves.
  • Queens (Q) are worth 12.
  • Jacks (J) are worth 11.
  • Aces (A) are worth 1.
  • All number cards (2-10) are at their face value.

You can only play cards that are fully uncovered. Click on two uncovered cards that sum to 13 to remove them. For example, you can pair a 6 with a 7, or a Jack (11) with a 2.

If you run out of available matches in the pyramid, click the stock pile at the bottom to draw a new card. You can cycle through the stock pile as many times as you need. The key is to think strategically to uncover new cards and clear the entire board. Good luck!
