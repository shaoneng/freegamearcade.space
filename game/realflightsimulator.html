How to Play Real Flight Simulator

Get ready to soar through the skies in Real Flight Simulator! Mastering your aircraft begins with a proper takeoff sequence. First, you can turn on your aircraft's lights with the Z key, but be aware this may cause lag. Next, lower your flaps and slats by pressing the X key for better lift. Ignite your engines with the C key and wait for them to fully start up. Throttle up by pressing and holding R or V, and once you're ready, release the parking brakes with the B key.

As you accelerate down the runway, keep an eye on your speed. Once you reach 150 knots, gently pull back by pressing the Down Arrow key to rotate and lift off the ground. After you've climbed above 100 feet, retract your landing gear with the M key. Congratulations, you're flying!

Once airborne, use the W and S keys to control your pitch (nose up/down) and the A and D keys to roll. Use Q and E for yaw (turning the nose left/right without banking). Adjust your engine power with the R (increase) and F (decrease) keys. For combat, switch weapons with the 1 key, lock onto targets with 2, and fire with the Spacebar. You can hide the controls info with H or return to the main menu with Escape or F2.
