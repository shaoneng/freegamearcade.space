Game Objective

Solitaire is a classic card game where the goal is to sort all 52 cards into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King. Every card you successfully place feels like a small win, clearing your mind as you clear the board.

How to Play

  1. Start the Game: Click “New Game” to shuffle the deck and lay out the seven tableau piles.
  2. Move Cards: Drag a face-up card (or a sequence of cards) onto another tableau pile. The move is valid if the card you're moving is one rank lower and of the opposite color (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7).
  3. Build Foundations: When an Ace is available, move it to one of the four foundation slots at the top. Build upon these foundations in ascending order (Ace, 2, 3, ... King) for each suit.
  4. Use the Stock Pile: If you run out of moves, click the stock pile to draw a new card (this version uses the Draw 1 rule, making it more accessible).

Tips for a Winning Strategy

  • Focus on Tableau Cards: Prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards in the tableau. This opens up more possibilities.
  • Free the Aces: Your first goal should be to find and move Aces to the foundation piles as soon as possible.
  • King's Corner: Try not to empty a tableau column unless you have a King ready to move into the empty space.
  • Don't Hesitate to Undo: If a move leads to a dead end, use the "Undo" button and explore a different path.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire a game of luck or skill?
It's a combination of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but your decisions on which cards to move, when to draw from the stock, and how to uncover hidden cards are pure skill. A skilled player can often win even with a difficult shuffle.

What is the difference between Draw 1 and Draw 3?
Draw 1 reveals one card from the stock pile at a time, making the game easier. Draw 3 is more challenging as it reveals three cards, but you can only play the top one. This version uses the friendly Draw 1 rule.
