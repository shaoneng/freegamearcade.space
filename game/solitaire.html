Solitaire

How to Play Solitaire

"Emma's afternoon hit a wall at exactly 3:17 PM. Her inbox chimed again, another urgent task from her manager. Minimized the spreadsheet and opened Solitaire. The familiar green-felt background blinked to life. She took a slow sip of her iced latte. The first shuffle felt like a deep breath. One by one, she moved cards to the foundation, each small stack a tiny win. Lost in the shuffle of aces and kings, she almost missed the freight train that rumbled past her window. When her fifteen-minute break ended, the last card flipped into place. A perfect sweep. She leaned back, chest light, and reopened that spreadsheet. The numbers looked friendlier now."

Just like Emma, you can step away from the buzz and dive into the calming world of Solitaire. It's a fun card game where you sort all 52 cards into four piles, one for each suit, from Ace to King.

Game Objective

The goal is to build four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order (Ace, 2, 3, ... King). You win when all 52 cards are correctly placed in the foundation piles.

How to Play

Click "New Game" to shuffle and deal the cards into seven tableau piles. You can drag a face-up card onto another tableau pile if it's the next lowest rank and an alternating color (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7). Click the stock pile to draw new cards. Move Aces to the foundation piles to start building your stacks.

Tips for a Better Game

  • Try to find and move Aces and Twos to the foundation piles as early as possible.
  • Prioritize moves that reveal face-down cards in the tableau.
  • Avoid emptying a tableau space unless you have a King to place there.
  • Use the "Undo" button to backtrack if a move doesn't work out.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire skill or luck?
It's a mix of both. Skill helps you make strategic choices, but the initial shuffle is all about luck. Good skills can help you win even with a tough layout.

Is Draw 1 or Draw 3 easier?
Draw 1 is easier, making it great for beginners. This version uses the Draw 1 rule.

Can I undo a move?
Yes, you can click the "Undo" button to reverse your last action and "Redo" to make it again.

Is this game free?
Absolutely. This Solitaire game is 100% free, with no sign-ups or downloads required.

Is it mobile-friendly?
Yes, the game is fully responsive and works great on both desktop and mobile devices using a mouse or touch controls.
