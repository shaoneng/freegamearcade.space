Game Objective

Solitaire is a classic one-player card game. Your goal is to sort all 52 cards into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King. Every card you clear feels like a small victory.

How to Play

Click "New Game" to shuffle and lay out seven tableau piles. Drag a face-up card (or a sequence of cards) onto another pile if it's the opposite color and one rank lower (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7). Click Aces to move them to the foundation piles at the top, followed by 2s, 3s, and so on. Click the stock pile to draw new cards. Continue until all cards are in the foundation piles or no moves are left.

How to Win

You win the game by successfully moving every card into the four foundation piles, with each suit stacked correctly from Ace to King. The game ends when the tableau and stock are empty.

Tips for a Winning Hand

  • Focus on finding and moving Aces and Twos to the foundation early.
  • Prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards in the tableau.
  • Try not to empty a tableau column unless you have a King to place there.
  • Don't be afraid to use the "Undo" button to explore different move sequences.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire skill or luck?
It's a mix of both. Skill lies in deciding which cards to move and when to draw from the stock. Luck comes from the initial shuffle. Good strategy can help you win even with a difficult deal.

What is Draw 1 vs. Draw 3?
Draw 1 reveals one card from the stock at a time and is generally easier. Draw 3 is more challenging. This version uses the Draw 1 rule to help you relax and win more easily.

Can I undo a move?
Yes, simply click the "Undo" button to reverse your last action and the "Redo" button to bring it back.
