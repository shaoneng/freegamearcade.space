Game Objective

Solitaire is a single-player card game where the main goal is to sort all 52 cards into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King.

How to Play

  • Tableau: Seven piles of cards are laid out. The top card of each is face-up.
  • Stockpile: The remaining cards form a face-down pile you can draw from.
  • Foundation: Four empty piles where you will build your suits from Ace to King.

Click "New Game" to start. Drag a face-up card (or a sequence) onto another tableau pile if it's the opposite color and one rank lower (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7). Click the stockpile to draw new cards one by one. Move Aces to the foundation piles to start building them up to the King.

How to Win

You win by moving every card into the four foundation piles, each stacked from Ace through King. A game ends when no cards remain on the tableau, stock, or waste piles.

Solitaire FAQ

Is Solitaire a game of skill or luck?

Solitaire combines both. It takes skill to decide which moves to make, when to draw from the stock, and how to uncover hidden cards. But it also takes some luck as the initial shuffle affects how many cards are accessible early on.

What are some good strategies for beginners?

First, try to find the aces and twos. Then, prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards. Try not to empty a pile if you don't have a King ready to place. Use the undo button to try different moves if you get stuck.

Is Draw 1 or Draw 3 easier?

Draw 1 is easier, which is what this version uses. Draw 3 is more challenging. With Draw 1, you can clear cards more easily and have a more relaxing experience.

Can I undo a move?

Yes. Click the Undo button at any time to reverse your last move. Click on redo to move it again.
