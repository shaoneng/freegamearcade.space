Solitaire is a fun card game where you sort all 52 cards into four piles, one for each suit, from Ace up to King. Every time you clear a card, it feels like a small victory.

How to Play

Solitaire is a one-player card game. You start with a shuffled deck and move cards between piles following simple rules. Your goal is to build four foundation piles from Ace up to King.

  1. Click "New Game" to shuffle and lay out seven tableau piles.
  2. Drag a face-up card (or a group in sequence) onto another tableau pile if it alternates color and steps down in rank (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7).
  3. Click Aces to move them to the foundation piles at the top, then add 2, 3, 4, and so on in each suit.
  4. Click the stock pile to reveal new cards one by one.
  5. Keep going until all cards are in the foundation piles or no more moves are possible.

Winning the Game

You win by moving every card into the four foundation piles (one per suit), each stacked from Ace through King. A game ends when no cards remain on the tableau, stock, or waste pile.

Solitaire FAQ

Is Solitaire a game of skill or luck?

It's a combination of both. It takes skill to decide which moves to make, when to draw from the stock, and how to uncover hidden cards. But luck plays a role too, as the initial shuffle can determine how accessible key cards are. With practice, you can improve your skills to overcome tough shuffles.

What are the best strategies for Solitaire?

First, try to find the aces and twos. Prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards. Try not to empty a tableau pile unless you have a King ready to place there. Don't be afraid to use the undo button to try different moves if you get stuck.

Is Draw 1 or Draw 3 better?

Draw 1 is generally easier, while Draw 3 is more challenging. This version of Solitaire uses the Draw 1 approach, making it more accessible to clear cards.

Can I undo a move?

Yes. Click the "Undo" button at any time to reverse your last move. You can also click "Redo" to perform the move again.

Is this game free to play?

Can I play Solitaire offline?

Does this game work on mobile?
