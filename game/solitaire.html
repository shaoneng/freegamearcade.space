Solitaire

How to Play Solitaire

The goal of Solitaire is to sort all 52 cards into four foundation piles, one for each suit, stacked from Ace up to King.

To start, click “New Game.” This will shuffle the deck and lay out seven tableau piles.

You can drag a face-up card, or a sequence of cards, onto another tableau pile if it alternates color and steps down in rank (for example, a red 6 can be placed on a black 7). As you uncover them, click Aces to move them to the top foundation area. Then, continue building these foundation piles by adding cards from 2 up to King in each respective suit.

If you run out of moves on the tableau, click the stock pile to reveal new cards one by one. Keep moving cards until all cards are in the foundation piles or no more moves are possible.

You win the game by successfully moving every single card into its correct foundation pile. The game concludes when no cards remain on the tableau, stock, or waste piles.

For an easier experience, this version of Solitaire uses the Draw 1 approach. Don't worry if you make a mistake; you can click the 'Undo' button at any time to reverse your last move. Solitaire is a free online game that requires an internet connection and is fully responsive for both desktop and mobile play, using either touch or mouse controls.
