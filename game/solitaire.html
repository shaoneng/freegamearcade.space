Solitaire

How to Play Solitaire

The main objective in Solitaire is to move all 52 cards to the four foundation piles at the top of the screen. Each foundation pile represents a suit (Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs, Spades) and must be built in ascending order, starting from the Ace and ending with the King.

The game begins with seven columns of cards, known as the tableau. You can move face-up cards onto another column if the card being moved is one rank lower and of the opposite color (e.g., a red 5 can be placed on a black 6). You can also move entire sequences of cards together. The goal is to uncover the face-down cards in the tableau.

If you run out of moves in the tableau, click on the stock pile in the top-left corner to draw a new card. Empty columns in the tableau can only be filled with a King. As Aces become available, move them to the foundation piles to begin building your stacks.

You win the game when all cards have been successfully moved to their respective foundation piles, and the tableau and stock are empty. If you get stuck with no possible moves left, you can start a new game.
