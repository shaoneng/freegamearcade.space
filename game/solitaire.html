Game Objective

Solitaire is a classic single-player card game. The goal is to sort a full 52-card deck into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King. Clearing the deck is a small victory that feels great every time.

How to Play

  • Start a Game: Click "New Game" to shuffle the deck and deal the cards into seven tableau piles.
  • Move Cards: Drag face-up cards onto another pile if they are in descending order and alternate in color (e.g., a red 8 on a black 9). You can move a single card or a sequence of cards.
  • Use the Stock Pile: If you're out of moves, click the stock pile to draw a new card. This version uses a "Draw 1" rule, making it easier to find playable cards.
  • Build Foundations: Move Aces to the four foundation slots at the top. Then, stack cards of the same suit on top in ascending order (2, 3, 4, and so on, up to the King).

Winning the Game

You win when all 52 cards are correctly placed in the four foundation piles. The game ends when the tableau and stock piles are empty.

Tips and Tricks

  • Uncover Hidden Cards: Always prioritize moves that flip over a face-down card in the tableau. This opens up new possibilities.
  • Free Up Your Aces: Try to find and move Aces to the foundation as soon as possible.
  • Empty Piles Strategically: Don't rush to empty a tableau column unless you have a King to place in it.
  • Use the Undo Button: If a move leads to a dead end, use the Undo button to backtrack and try a different strategy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire skill or luck?
It's a mix of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but your strategy and decision-making are pure skill. A good player can win even with a challenging shuffle.

Is this Draw 1 or Draw 3?
This version uses the easier Draw 1 rule, where you flip one card at a time from the stock pile.

Is the game free to play?
Yes, this Solitaire game is 100% free, with no sign-ups or downloads needed.

Can I play on my phone?
Absolutely. The game is designed to work perfectly on both desktop and mobile devices.
