What is Solitaire?

Solitaire is a single-player card game where the goal is to sort a full 52-card deck into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King. It’s a puzzle of foresight and strategy, offering a satisfying sense of order with every card you place correctly.

How to Play

  • The Setup: The game starts by dealing cards into seven piles on the tableau. The first pile has one card, the second has two, and so on, up to seven. Only the top card of each pile is face-up. The rest of the cards form the stock pile.
  • Moving Cards: You can move a face-up card onto another tableau pile if it's the next lowest rank and the opposite color (e.g., a black 6 on a red 7). You can also move a sequence of cards together.
  • Using the Stock: Click the stock pile to draw cards (this version uses Draw 1). These cards can be played onto the tableau or the foundation piles.
  • Building Foundations: The goal is to build four foundation piles, one for each suit, starting with the Ace and going up to the King. Once an Ace is free, you can move it to a foundation spot.

How to Win

You win the game by successfully moving all 52 cards into their respective foundation piles, stacked from Ace to King. A game ends when the tableau, stock, and waste piles are all empty.

Tips for Winning

  • Uncover Hidden Cards: Prioritize moves that will flip over a face-down card in the tableau. This opens up new possibilities.
  • Find Aces and Twos: Free up Aces and Twos as early as you can to start building your foundation piles.
  • King's Corner: Try not to empty a tableau column unless you have a King ready to place there. An empty spot can only be filled by a King.
  • Use Undo: Don't be afraid to use the undo button to backtrack from a move that didn't work out.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire skill or luck?

It's a combination of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but your decisions on which cards to move and when to draw from the stock require skill. A good player can often win even with a difficult deal.

Is Draw 1 or Draw 3 easier?

Draw 1 is generally easier as it cycles through the stock pile more quickly, giving you access to all the cards. This version uses the Draw 1 rule to provide a more relaxing experience.

Can I undo a move?

Yes. This game includes an "Undo" button that allows you to reverse your last move if you make a mistake or want to try a different strategy.
