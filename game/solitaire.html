Game Objective

Solitaire is a classic single-player card game. The goal is to sort a shuffled 52-card deck into four foundation piles—one for each suit—in ascending order from Ace to King. Clearing the deck is a satisfying challenge of strategy and a little bit of luck.

The Rules

  • Start a Game: Click “New Game” to shuffle the deck and lay out seven piles, known as the tableau.
  • Move Cards: You can move a face-up card onto another tableau pile if it's one rank lower and of the opposite color (e.g., a red 6 onto a black 7). You can also move a whole sequence of cards together.
  • Build Foundations: When an Ace is uncovered, click it to move it to a foundation pile at the top. From there, you'll add the 2, 3, 4, and so on, up to the King for each suit.
  • Use the Stock Pile: If you run out of moves, click the stock pile to reveal new cards one by one. This version uses the "Draw 1" rule, which makes it a bit friendlier for beginners.

How to Win

You win the game by successfully moving all 52 cards into the four foundation piles, with each pile correctly stacked by suit from Ace to King. The game is over when no cards remain on the tableau or in the stock pile.

Tips for a Better Game

  • Uncover Hidden Cards: Always prioritize moves that will flip over a face-down card in the tableau. More cards mean more options.
  • Free the Aces: Your primary goal should be to find and move the Aces to the foundation as soon as possible.
  • Manage Empty Piles: Try not to empty a tableau column unless you have a King ready to place there. Only a King can start a new pile in an empty slot.
  • Use the Undo Button: Don't be afraid to backtrack if you get stuck. The undo button lets you try different moves to find the best path forward.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire skill or luck?
It's a combination of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but how you play the hand requires skill. Smart decisions can overcome a tough shuffle.

What's the difference between Draw 1 and Draw 3?
Draw 1 reveals one card at a time from the stock pile, which is generally easier. Draw 3 is more challenging. This version uses the simpler Draw 1 rule to help you clear cards more easily.

Can I undo a move?
Yes. Click the Undo button at any time to reverse your last move. You can also click Redo to bring it back.
