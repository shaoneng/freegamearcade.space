Solitaire

About & How to Play Solitaire

Sometimes, all it takes is a quiet moment and a deck of cards to turn the tide of a long workday. Just like our player Emma, you can step away from the daily buzz and dive into the calming world of Solitaire. A perfect sweep of the cards can feel like conquering something much bigger. This classic card game is all about sorting 52 cards into four piles, one for each suit, from Ace to King.

Game Objective

The goal is to move all 52 cards into four "foundation" piles at the top of the screen. Each foundation must be built up by suit, starting with the Ace and ending with the King.

How to Play

  • Start a Game: Click "New Game" to shuffle and deal the cards into seven tableau piles.
  • Move Cards: Drag a face-up card onto another tableau pile if it's the opposite color and one rank lower (e.g., a red 6 onto a black 7). You can also move a sequence of cards together.
  • Build Foundations: Click an Ace to move it to a foundation pile. Then, add the 2, 3, and so on for that suit.
  • Use the Stock: Click the stock pile in the corner to reveal new cards one at a time.

How to Win

You win by successfully moving every card from the tableau and stock into the four foundation piles, completely clearing the board. A game ends when all cards are sorted or when no more moves are possible.

Quick Tips for Success

  • Prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards.
  • Try to find and move Aces and Twos to the foundation as soon as possible.
  • Avoid emptying a tableau pile unless you have a King to place there.
  • Use the "Undo" button to backtrack if a move doesn't work out.

Solitaire FAQ

Is Solitaire a game of skill or luck?

It's a combination of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but your decisions on which cards to move and when to draw from the stock require skill. Good strategy can help you win even with a tough deal.

What is the difference between Draw 1 and Draw 3?

Draw 1 reveals one card at a time from the stock, which is generally easier. Draw 3 reveals three cards and is more challenging. This version uses the Draw 1 rule to provide a more relaxing experience.

Can I undo a move?

Yes, you can click the "Undo" button at any time to reverse your last action. You can also use the "Redo" button to perform the move again.
