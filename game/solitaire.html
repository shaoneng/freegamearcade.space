How to Play Solitaire

Solitaire is a classic single-player card game. The goal is to sort a shuffled 52-card deck into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King.

Game Setup & Moves:

  • Start the Game: Click “New Game” to shuffle and lay out the cards into seven tableau piles. The last card of each pile is face-up.
  • Moving Cards: Drag a face-up card (or a sequence of cards) onto another tableau pile. The move is valid only if the card you're moving is one rank lower and of the opposite color (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7).
  • Uncovering Cards: Moving a card from the tableau will flip over the face-down card beneath it. Prioritize moves that uncover new cards.
  • The Stock Pile: If you run out of moves, click the stock pile in the top-left corner to draw a new card into the waste pile. This version uses a "Draw 1" rule, making it easier to find playable cards.
  • Building Foundations: When an Ace becomes available, move it to one of the four foundation slots at the top. Build on these foundations by adding the 2, 3, 4, and so on, of the same suit, all the way up to the King.

How to Win:

You win the game by successfully moving all 52 cards into the four foundation piles. A game ends when no cards are left on the tableau, stock, or waste pile.

Solitaire FAQ

Is Solitaire a game of skill or luck?
It's a combination of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but your decisions on which cards to move, when to draw from the stock, and how to uncover hidden cards require skill. Good strategy can help you win even with a tough shuffle.

What are some tips to get better at Solitaire?
First, try to expose Aces and Twos. Prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards to give you more options. Avoid emptying a tableau column unless you have a King ready to place there. And don't forget to use the Undo button to backtrack if a move doesn't pan out!

Can I undo a move?
Yes. Click the "Undo" button anytime to reverse your last action. You can also click "Redo" to perform the move again.
