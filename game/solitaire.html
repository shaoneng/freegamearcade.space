Solitaire: Rules, Tips, and a Bit of Luck

"Sometimes, all it takes is a quiet moment and a deck of cards to turn the tide of a long workday." This sentiment from a fellow player perfectly captures the magic of Solitaire. Like them, you can step away from the daily buzz and find a moment of calm focus. Dive in and see how a simple deck of cards can brighten any day.

How to Play

Solitaire is a single-player card game where your goal is to sort a 52-card deck into four foundation piles, one for each suit, in ascending order from Ace to King.

  • Setup: A new game begins by shuffling and dealing cards into seven tableau piles.
  • Moving Cards: Drag a face-up card (or a sequence of cards) onto another tableau pile if it's of the opposite color and one rank lower (e.g., a red 6 on a black 7).
  • Foundations: Move Aces to the four foundation spots at the top. Build on them by adding cards of the same suit in ascending order (2, 3, 4, up to King).
  • Stock Pile: Click the stock pile to draw new cards. In this version, you'll draw one card at a time.

How to Win

You win the game when you have successfully moved all 52 cards into the four foundation piles, completely clearing the tableau and stock piles.

Top Tips for Winning

  • Uncover Cards: Prioritize moves that flip over face-down cards in the tableau.
  • Find Aces: Locate and move Aces to the foundation as soon as possible.
  • King's Corner: Try not to empty a tableau column unless you have a King to place there.
  • Use Undo: Don't be afraid to use the "Undo" button to explore different move sequences if you get stuck.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire skill or luck?

It’s a mix of both. The initial shuffle is luck, but your decisions on which cards to move and when to draw from the stock require skill. Good strategy can help you win even with a tough shuffle.

Is this version Draw 1 or Draw 3?

This version uses the Draw 1 rule, where you flip one card at a time from the stock. This is generally considered easier and more beginner-friendly than Draw 3.
