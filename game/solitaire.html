Solitaire is a fun card game where you sort all 52 cards into four piles, one for each suit, from Ace up to King. Every time you clear a card, it feels like a small victory. If you're a newbie, don't worry. We've explained everything you need to know below.

How to Play

Solitaire is a one-player card game. You start with a shuffled deck and move cards between piles following simple rules. Your goal is to build four foundation piles, one for each suit, from Ace up to King.

  1. Click “New Game” to shuffle and lay out seven tableau piles.
  2. Drag a face-up card (or a group in sequence) onto another tableau pile if it alternates color and steps down in rank (for example, a red 6 on a black 7).
  3. Click Aces to move them to the foundation piles at the top (or use auto-collect), then add 2s, 3s, and so on up to the King for each suit.
  4. Click the stock pile to reveal new cards one by one.
  5. Keep moving cards until all are in the foundation piles or no more moves are possible.

How to Win

You win by moving every card into the four foundation piles (one per suit), each stacked in ascending order from Ace through King. A game is won when no cards remain on the tableau, stock, or waste piles.

Strategy & Tips

First, try to find the aces and twos. Then, prioritize moves that uncover face-down cards. Try not to empty a tableau pile unless you have a King ready to place there. Don't forget to use the undo button to try different moves and backtrack if you get stuck.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Solitaire a game of skill or luck?

Solitaire combines both. It takes skill to decide which moves to make, when to draw from the stock, and how to uncover hidden cards. But it also takes some luck, as the initial shuffle affects which cards are accessible early on. A tough shuffle can make a round hard to win, but you can improve your skills to overcome it.

Is Draw 1 or Draw 3 easier?

Draw 1 is easier, while Draw 3 is more challenging. The version on this page uses the Draw 1 approach, so you can clear cards more easily.

Can I undo a move?

Yes. Click the "Undo" button at any time to reverse your last move. You can also click "Redo" to perform the move again.
